# LHC v0.2 — pre-registered decision table

**Locked:** 2026-05-09 (before any v0.2 model run; before authoring of v0.2 tasks completes).
**Source of these thresholds:** external review of 2026-05-08, accepted in full. See [`docs/journal/2026-05-08-external-review-and-decontamination.md`](../../docs/journal/2026-05-08-external-review-and-decontamination.md).
**Why pre-registered:** without committing to thresholds before seeing the data, we will rationalize whatever result we get. This document is the contract.

> **Do not edit this file after the v0.2 sweep starts.** If a threshold turns out to be wrong, write a *new* decision document for v0.3 with a `Reason for revision` section. The old contract stays in place as the historical record.

---

## 1. Primary endpoint

- **Benchmark:** LHC v0.2, frozen at the commit tagged `lhc-v0.2-frozen`.
- **Primary gap mode:** `current` (the production-relevant condition — agent-task-style chatter).
- **Primary metrics:**
  - LHC v0.2 **overall** mean (range 0.0–2.0, sum of correctness ∈ {0,1} + meta_awareness ∈ {0,1}).
  - LHC v0.2 **resumption** category mean.
- **Artifact check (secondary gap mode):** `neutral` (lorem-ipsum filler). Used to distinguish "model is generally bad at long context" from "model is hijacked by gap-style content."
- **Diagnostic gap modes:** `none` and `placeholder` (run for transparency, not used in primary gates).

## 2. Comparison structure

- **Trials per cell:** 3 trials × {model × gap mode}.
- **Pairing:** all comparisons are *paired by (task_id, gap_mode, trial_index)*. Same task, same gap, same trial-index → directly comparable. Trial 1 of model A on `current` `resumption_004` vs trial 1 of model B on `current` `resumption_004`, etc.
- **Aggregation:** mean of paired per-task scores, with a 95% bootstrap confidence interval over tasks (bootstrap resamples drawn at the *task* level, holding gap_mode and trial_index fixed; 10000 resamples). The CI for a delta is the CI for the per-task mean of (model_A_score − model_B_score).
- **Judge:** Claude Opus 4.7. Used for all model evaluations and for the judge-stability audit (§5).

## 3. Gates: "Ember v0.1.5 iter-900 is genuinely better than base Qwen3-8B"

Convincing **only if all** of the following hold, computed on LHC v0.2:

- (G1) `current` gap **overall**: Ember mean ≥ Qwen mean + **0.20**
- (G2) `current` gap **resumption** category: Ember mean ≥ Qwen mean + **0.30**
- (G3) `neutral` gap **overall**: Ember mean ≥ Qwen mean + **0.10**
- (G4) Paired bootstrap **95% CI lower bound** for the `current` overall delta (Ember − Qwen) is **> 0.00**

If Ember only wins on `placeholder` (the literal training-data gap string) but not on `current` or `neutral`, this is **not** evidence of generalization — it's evidence the model has memorized the training-data placeholder. **Ignore `placeholder` wins.**

## 4. Gates: "Ember v0.1.5 iter-900 is genuinely worse than Ministral-8B-2512"

Convincing **only if all** of the following hold:

- (G5) `current` gap **overall**: Ministral mean ≥ Ember mean + **0.20**
- (G6) `neutral` gap **overall**: Ministral mean ≥ Ember mean + **0.15**
- (G7) `current` gap **resumption** category: Ministral mean ≥ Ember mean + **0.30**
- (G8) **No** gap mode shows Ember ahead of Ministral by ≥ **0.10**

If Ministral wins **only** on `current` (and Ember matches or beats on `none`/`neutral`/`placeholder`), call it **gap-style sensitivity in Ember**, not general inferiority. The fix in that case is distractor-style training data, not switching base.

## 5. Gates: "LHC v0.2 itself is insufficient"

Declare LHC v0.2 unfit for shipping claims (regardless of where Ember lands) if **any** of these hold:

- (G9) **Judge instability**: rejudge a 10% audit sample (≥ 3 task-trials per model) with the same Opus 4.7 judge in a fresh API session. If any model's overall mean shifts by ≥ **0.10** under rejudge, the judge is too noisy at this granularity.
- (G10) **Gap-mode rank inversion**: model rankings on `neutral` differ from rankings on `current` by **more than 2 positions** (e.g. a model #1 on neutral that drops to #4 on current). Indicates the benchmark is dominated by gap sensitivity rather than capability.
- (G11) **CI overlap**: 95% paired-bootstrap CIs for *both* the Ember/Qwen overall delta AND the Ember/Ministral overall delta include deltas of **±0.15**. If we can't even tell who's better at that magnitude, the benchmark lacks discriminative power.
- (G12) **Manual audit miss-rate**: hand-audit the full prompt + full response + judge reasoning for 10% of task-trials (≥ 9 task-trials at N=24). If ≥ **5%** of audited labels are *materially wrong* in a direction that would flip a per-task score, the judge or rubric is too unreliable. ("Materially wrong" = a judge call we'd score differently after reading the full text.)
- (G13) **Variance attribution**: if a one-way ANOVA-style decomposition shows `gap_mode` accounts for **more variance than `model_id`** in overall scores across all cells, the benchmark is measuring distractor sensitivity more than model capability.

## 6. Decision matrix (action per outcome)

Read top to bottom; the first matching row wins.

| If the gates say… | Action |
|-------------------|--------|
| **Any of G9–G13 trips** (LHC v0.2 itself is insufficient) | **Stop.** Do not ship. Do not invest more training cycles. Build a different evaluation: hybrid of LongBench / RULER / 8–12 hand-built held-out *product* tasks. Re-decide everything from there. |
| **G1–G4 fail** (Ember not better than base) | **Stop the v0.1.5 line.** Whatever Ember v0.1.5 is doing, it is not measurably improving on base Qwen3-8B in a clean comparison. Either retrain on a different recipe (more distractor-style data, different rank/modules) or pivot base. Do not publish v0.1. |
| **G1–G4 pass AND G5–G8 pass** (Ember beats base, loses to Ministral) | Two sub-options, choose by reading the failure pattern: (a) if Ember's resumption gap is gap-style-only (G7 holds but G6 doesn't), train against distractors and re-test; (b) if Ember loses on `neutral` too, switch base model (Mistral 8B-class with native long context, or Qwen3-8B + YaRN re-evaluated). Do not publish v0.1. |
| **G1–G4 pass AND G5–G8 fail** (Ember beats base, NOT worse than Ministral by our thresholds) | **Validate on held-out product tasks** (≥ 8 real agent workflows; see OQ-01 in journal). If product tasks agree, publish as `slowlitlabs/ember-v0.1` with a model card that states the LHC v0.2 numbers, the published methodology, and the held-out product results. Do not publish before product validation. |
| **G1–G4 pass AND G5–G8 ambiguous** (one or two of G5–G8 trip but not all) | Treat as Ember tied with Ministral. Same path as the row above: held-out product tasks decide. |

## 7. Operational protocol

### Run order

1. **Methodology fixes land first** (stable hashing, full-message scorecards, fail-fast lifecycle) — completed 2026-05-09 ahead of this document.
2. **LHC v0.2 tasks authored** per [`AUTHORING.md`](AUTHORING.md), 24 tasks total, frozen at commit tag `lhc-v0.2-frozen`.
3. **Deterministic resume-state validator baseline** built in parallel (~2-3 hours, see journal D-02 step 7). This is one Python file that solves the resumption tasks via schema validation, with no LLM. Used to answer "is fine-tuning chasing something a 50-line parser would also solve?"
4. **Sweep:** 4 models × 4 gap modes × 3 trials = 48 scorecards.
   - Models: Ember v0.1.5 iter-900 (mlx local), Qwen3-8B base (OpenRouter for stable hosting), Ministral-8B-2512 (OpenRouter), Llama-3.1-8B (OpenRouter).
   - Gap modes: `none`, `placeholder`, `neutral`, `current`.
   - Trials: 3.
5. **Compute gates G1–G13** via a single analysis script (yet to be written, will land before sweep) that consumes the 48 scorecards and emits a pass/fail report against this document. No interactive judgment in the analysis — the script's output IS the verdict.
6. **Manual audit** (G12): sample 10% of task-trials uniformly across (model, gap_mode), read the full prompt + response + judge reasoning, hand-rate. Compare to recorded scores.
7. **Decision** per §6 matrix.

### What we will NOT do

- **No new training during this cycle.** We are evaluating what we already have. Training before evaluating is how we got into the v0.1.5 mess.
- **No goalpost adjustment.** If the gates don't fire as expected, we go to "LHC v0.2 insufficient" or "Ember not better than base," not "let's loosen the threshold."
- **No selective reporting.** All 48 scorecards published, all gate computations published, regardless of which row of §6 we land in.

### What "shipping" means

For this cycle, shipping means: pushing weights to `slowlitlabs/ember-v0.1` on HuggingFace with a model card. The model card must include:
- LHC v0.2 numbers across all gap modes
- Per-gate result (G1 through G13, pass/fail)
- The 48 scorecards in `evals/results/published/lhc-v0.2/`
- A link to this DECISION.md file as it stood at sweep time
- The held-out product task results (OQ-01)

We do **not** ship if any of:
- Any G9–G13 fires
- G1–G4 fail
- Held-out product validation disagrees with LHC v0.2

## 9. Glossary

- **paired bootstrap CI**: standard nonparametric confidence interval. Resample tasks (with replacement) 10000 times, compute the per-task mean of (model_A − model_B) on each resample, take the 2.5th and 97.5th percentiles as the 95% CI.
- **rank inversion**: change in the ranked order of models when sorted by mean overall score. "More than 2 positions" means a model that's #N on one gap mode is at #N+3 or further on the other.
- **materially wrong** (G12): a judge label that, after reading the full prompt + response, the human auditor would assign differently. "Different score" means at least one of correctness or meta_awareness flips.
